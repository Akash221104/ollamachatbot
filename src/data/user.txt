What is DRP
A disaster recovery plan (DRP) is a documented, structured approach with instructions for responding to unplanned incidents. This step-by-step plan consists of the precautions to minimize the effects of a disaster so that the ADV system  can continue to operate or quickly resume its mission-critical functions. Disaster recovery and business continuity planning are integral parts of the overall risk management for an organization in this case ADV system. Both processes are equally important because they provide detailed strategies on how the business will continue after severe interruptions and disasters.

Scenarios when DRP needs to be invoked
Following are the various circumstances where DRP needs to be activated
Catastrophic events resulting in a major disruption of services and complete processing capability cannot be achieved for a substantial period of time. In these cases, recovery will require the use of alternate processing sites as well as off-site offices for employees over an extended period of time. Some examples of such events are war, earthquakes, acts of terrorism, etc.
Disastrous events where overall business infrastructure may experience a severe disaster resulting in the total shut down and complete processing capability of all business processes may be down. Further, key personnel may not be able to access the premises and also lead to non-availability of key resources. Some examples of such events are floods, rain, fire, riots, power shutdown, etc.

Key Assumptions/Prerequisites
Following are the assumptions in case of DR Plan:
Connectivity of the DR site is live, with two links each of — Megabits per second, and sufficient to support the additional load created by the restored processes.
The Network Operating Center (NOC) of NDC Delhi and Pune  has its own disaster recovery mechanisms.
DR site contains sufficient IT infrastructure: e.g. required Servers, all types of required connectivity, network equipment and Desktop(s) along with a fully functional internal LAN.
In the case of major mishaps like flooding, earthquake, etc. both Primary and DR sites are not affected at the same time.
In the case of network data corruption or virus attack, which shall be a remote possibility, both DR and DC may be shut down together.
No single point of failure for network infrastructure at DC and DR sites. There shall be multiple network connectivity between DC and DR site, as well as from clients to the DR site.
Failback time may depend on the time taken by DGS to regularize the resources especially in case of major disasters affecting the DC infrastructure.
For failover and failback, additional resources in terms of manpower and financial budget shall be facilitated.

·	The Authorized person is informed of Disaster.
·	Authorized person calls the command recipient (Project director in this case) and gives a command (DR invocation command in this case).
·	Command recipient (Project Director) callbacks the same authorized person on the contact number mentioned in DR Authority List and confirms command (invocation of DR).
·	On confirmation, the command recipient (Project Director) acts as per command (DR invocation).
This method of the authorization will now onwards be referred to as the “Call Back” Method in this Plan.

Activating the Disaster Recovery Plan
If a disaster occurs, the first priority is to ensure that all employees are safe and accounted for. After this, steps must be taken to mitigate any further damage to the facility and to reduce the impact of the disaster on the organization. Regardless of the category that the disaster falls into, dealing with a disaster can be broken down into the following steps:

Disaster identification and declaration
The incident of ADV service discontinuation is identified by the 1st level O&M team.  Whether it is a temporary service failure situation like temporary network breakdown, hardware failure, software failure is identified with the DR authority team. According to the incident, criticality decisions will be conveyed to the 2nd and 3rd level authorities. Disaster will be declared based on the probability rating (PR) and Impact rating (IR) of the incident. The details of PR and IR are mentioned in the Business Continuity plan.

DRP activation
Once the incident is identified as “Disaster Situation”, the DRP activation process will be initiated by level 2 and 3. During the Disaster activation process, the nature of the disaster, its impact is analyzed and accordingly DR action plan is initiated with concerned authority’s approval.

Communicating the disaster
The disaster situation is declared and communicated to all the related stakeholders. The estimated service reestablishment period (RTO) is communicated to all the stakeholders. During the failover activation process, if the incident cannot be handled by data backup from the tape library, migration of the service from DC to DR site is carried out, for that the network migration process is primarily important. The DR site network migration is initiated.

Assessment of current and prevention of further damage
Along with migration from DC to DR site, the assessment of damage such as asset failure and its replacement if required is analyzed. Any future damage to the asset is also analyzed and reported.

Standby facility activation
In case of disaster situation, where migration from primary to DR site is required, the DR site is activated ensuring consistent data state of the ADV system. The services are activated from the DR site. At DR site, generated data is stored locally at DR site and outage data backup is initiated on spare drives at DR site.

Establish IT operations
During the establishment of services from the DR site, along with client services, all the peripheral services like reporting, logging, etc (which were in the cold state normally) are also started from the DR site.

Repair and rebuilding of the primary facility
Once the disaster situation is recovered at the primary site, migration of services from secondary to primary is initiated, called failback operation; it's a planned operation. During failback operation, outage data backup is transferred from secondary to the primary site. Again network and site migration from secondary to the primary site is conducted.

DRP Activation
Once the Disaster Recovery Lead has formally declared that a disaster has occurred s/he will initiate the activation of the DRP by triggering the Disaster Recovery Call Tree. The following information will be provided in the calls that the Disaster Recovery Lead makes and should be passed during subsequent calls:
That a disaster has occurred
The nature of the disaster (if known)
The initial estimation of the magnitude of the disaster (if known)
The initial estimation of the impact of the disaster (if known)
The initial estimation of the expected duration of the disaster (if known)
Actions that have been taken to this point
Any other pertinent information
If the Disaster Recovery Lead is unavailable to trigger the Disaster Recovery Call Tree, that responsibility shall fall to the Disaster Management Team Lead.

Plan Maintenance Schedule

Basic Maintenance
The plan will be routinely evaluated once each year. The ADV Project team must review all portions of the plan. In addition, the plan will be tested on a regular basis and any faults will be corrected. Disaster Recovery Plan coordinator has the responsibility of overseeing the individual documents and files and ensuring that they meet standards and are consistent with the rest of the plan.

Change-Driven Maintenance
It is inevitable in the changing environment of the computer industry that this disaster recovery plan will become outdated and unusable unless someone keeps it up to date. Changes that will likely affect the plan fall into several categories:
Hardware changes
Software changes
System Configuration changes
Facility changes
Personnel changes
Procedural changes
As changes occur in any of the areas mentioned above, ADV Project team will determine if changes to the plan are necessary. This decision will require that the managers be familiar with the plan in some detail. A document referencing common changes that will require plan maintenance will be made available and updated when required.
The staff in the affected area will make changes that affect the platform recovery portions of the plan. After the changes have been made, the IT team will be advised that the updated documents are available. They will incorporate the changes into the body of the plan and distribute as required.

Changes Requiring Plan Maintenance
The following lists some of the types of changes that may require revisions to the Disaster Recovery Plan. Any change that can potentially affect whether the plan can be used to successfully restore the operations of the department's computer and network systems should be reflected in the plan. The change management team could be decided by the BCP management team or it could be itself only. Responsibility for the change management team is also given below.
Hardware
Additions, deletions, or upgrades to hardware platforms
Software
Additions, deletions, or upgrades to system software
System Configuration changes
Changes to applications software affected by the plan
Facilities
Changes that affect the availability/usability of the hot Site location
Personnel
Changes of personnel identified by name in the plan
Changes in the organizational structure of the department
Procedural Changes
Changes to off-site backup procedures, locations, etc.
Changes to application backups.
Changes to vendor lists maintained for acquisition and support purposes, etc.

Change Management Team
The primary responsibility of this team is to ensure that any changes being done on the production are simultaneously changed at the DR site. The change management process, as well as the team structure, is defined in detail in the “Software Configuration Management Plan”.

DC Primary Site Reactivation
After the failover operation, the DR site starts working in the active mode, handling the application requests. During this, the DC site has to undergo repair of the failed components. Once the repair of the DC site completes, the primary site reactivation process is initiated. This activity is known as fallback i.e. migration of services from DR site to DC site. Failback is a planned operation and requires service disruption.
During DR site working in the active mode, the data generated by application services is known as outage data. This outage data needs to be migrated to the DC site for functional reestablishment. The amount of data to be transferred to the DC site depends on disaster impact on the DC site infrastructure.  Few scenarios are listed below.

What is Fail-back?
Failback is the process of re-synchronizing that data back to the primary data center, halting I/O and application activity once again and cutting back over to the original location.

Following are the steps the DR and DC administrators have to be performed during the failback process.

DC site failure without data loss at DC site
Configure the database to generate archive files at a predefined location
Declared DR disruption.
Send the outage files (some archive files and an unarchived WAL file)

At DC site
If DC site failure with entire data loss at DC site
Extract the base backup.
Copy the extracted data directory to the database directory.
Mount DC site SAN disk to the database data directory.
Remove all files that are not required in recovery/warm-standby mode.
Add the files (such as recovery.conf) to the database data directory that are required in recovery/warm-standby mode.
Edit the configuration file (recovery.conf) for setting the database in warm standby mode.
Set the warm-standby mode archive files path in the configuration file (recovery.conf) of the data directory.
If archive_files directory is not present in the pg_xlog directory then create it in the pg_xlog directory, which will be used for storing archive files once the database comes in active mode. (pg_xlog directory is present in the database data directory)
Mount disk of DC-DR agent at DC to pg_xlog directory of the database data directory.
Set the archive files path in the configuration file (postgresql.conf) of the data directory for storing archive files once the database comes in active mode.
Copy and Replay all the outage archive files.
Replay the last unarchived WAL file. This triggers the database to come out of warm-standby mode.
Now DC is in active mode and ready to handle the incoming requests. To perform the initial or normal setup operations.

Copy and Replay all the outage archive files.
Replay the last unarchived WAL file. This triggers the database to come out of warm-standby mode.

Plan Testing & Maintenance
While efforts will be made initially to construct this DRP in as complete and accurate a manner as possible, it is essentially impossible to address all possible problems at any one time. Additionally, over time the Disaster Recovery needs of the enterprise will change. As a result of these two factors, this plan will need to be tested on a periodic basis to discover errors and omissions and will need to be maintained to address them.

Maintenance
The DRP will be updated once a year or any time a major system update or upgrade is performed, whichever is more often. The Disaster Recovery Lead will be responsible for updating the entire document, and so is permitted to request information and updates from other employees and departments within the organization in order to complete this task.
Maintenance of the plan will include (but is not limited to) the following:
Ensuring that call trees are up to date.
Ensuring that all team lists are up to date.
Reviewing the plan to ensure that all of the instructions are still relevant to the organization
Making any major changes and revisions in the plan to reflect shifts, changes, and goals.
Ensuring that the plan meets any requirements specified.
Other project specific maintenance goals
During the Maintenance periods, any changes to the Disaster Recovery Teams will be accounted for. If any member of a Disaster Recovery Team no longer works with the company, it will be the responsibility of the Disaster Recovery Lead to appoint a new team member and update the DR plan with the new member’s role in the project activity. In this case , names of the personnel involved in the project are listed in the Infrastructure Maintenance plan.

Testing
C-DAC and DGS are committed to ensuring that this DRP is functional. The DRP should be tested every year in order to ensure that it is still effective.
